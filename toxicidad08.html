3.8 OTHER TOXICITIES


Introduction


Some rare and infrequent immune-mediated toxicities have been described, based on reports of isolated cases.


Since the symptoms presented are so rare, attention by the attending physician as well as the patient is required in order to avoid serious adverse events and/or a fatal outcome in these cases. Early clinical management may prevent fatal events.


We refer to ocular toxicities (in the form of episcleritis, conjunctivitis and uveitis) described with treatment with Ipilimumab (anti-CTLA-4) in less than 1%.


In addition to haematological toxicities (such as autoimmune anaemia, immune-mediated thrombocytopenia and neutropenia, acquired haemophilia A, spinal cord aplasia and disseminated intravascular coagulation) described in less than 4% of cases with anti-TCLA-4 treatment; rheumatological pictures in the form of polymyositis and cardiac issues such as cardiomyositis with anti-PDL-1 and anti-PD-1 treatments (Nivolumab and Pembrolizumab) in less than 2%.



Diagnosis



       In the event of ocular toxicity, any changes in visual acuity, blurred vision and red eye...


       In the event of haematological toxicity, any changes in white blood cells, red blood cells or platelets Observed with bleeding in more severe cases.


       In the event of cardiac toxicity, any symptoms of congestive heart failure, acute pulmonary oedema, chest pain, palpitations, arrhythmia, etc.


       In the event of rheumatic toxicity, any symptoms of arthralgia and joint stiffness, proximal muscle weakness, asthenia, fever, among others.

Therapeutic management


Monitor the signs and symptoms of each of the toxicities and establish the grade according to the CTCAE version 4.03 (GRADE 0-5).


In the event of ocular toxicity, any changes in visual acuity, blurred vision, or red eye, intravenous (or equivalent). In refractory cases, the use of immunosuppressants is proposed, such as Cyclosporine, Immunoglobulin and transfusion of blood products. In cases where diagnosis is unclear, bone marrow biopsy is recommended.


In the case of immune-mediated eye toxicities, the most common symptoms are episcleritis, conjunctivitis and uveitis. Evaluation by an ophthalmology specialist is recommended in any case with topical corticosteroid treatment with 1% prednisolone acetate and a cycloplegic, reserving oral treatment (prednisone 1-2 mg/kg/day orally) for severe cases. The dose should not be reduced until there is an improvement to at least GRADE 1 and in a period of no less than 30 days. Treatment should be discontinued in cases of GRADE 3-4.


In the event of immune-mediated cytopenias it is recommended that treatment be discontinued. With this, the majority regress with the administration of prednisolone 1mg/kg/day.


In the event of heart toxicities in the form of cardiomyositis, evaluation by a cardiology specialist and intensive medicine specialist is recommended with a biochemical study (creatinine kinase, troponins), echocardiographic study, and immediate initiation of methylprednisolone 1-2 mg/kg/day intravenously is recommended. The majority of these patients present depressions in systolic function and cardio-muscular contractility capacity. Discontinuation of treatment is imperative.



REMEMBER :


• It is important to remember that the symptoms presented are non-specific and uncommon, so attention by the attending physician as well as the patient is required in order to avoid serious adverse events and/or a fatal evolution in these cases. Early clinical management may prevent fatal events.
